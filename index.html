ShutterBuddy

ShutterBuddy is an web app that enables you to build the photo album of your dreams -- no camera required! For our final project at The Iron Yard, I combined the Flickr and Google Maps APIs with a little Rails magic to help you create a customized, Creative Commons-licensed photo album based on location, landmarks, weather and time of day. At the end, you get a link to your album to share your fabulous trip with friends and family!

Battleship

My first major project at the Iron Yard, Battleship is a game that creates and displays the game board and lets you take turns playing the computer on the command line. The assignment helped me understand the basics of control flow, variables, and methods in Ruby, Object-Oriented Programming, the basic use cases of Git for single-developer projects and how test suites can assist in development.

Survey Possum

In Week 5, I worked another Ruby dev and a group from the Front End Engineering class to create a survey app. The app required users to be able to sign up and create their own surveys, with both questions to be asked and possible answers. Once the survey is complete, the author can send links to anyone with an e-mail address. Survey authors can look back at their surveys at any time to see submitted answers.
